FOR BEST QUALITY PRINT, PLEASE READ ALL(!) OF BELOW:

To print the Rukka Buggy, you need time or a resin printer ;-)
I REALLY recommend a layer high of 0.075mm or lower for all parts to get the quality I intended. 
Please see the pics, those are all printed in FDM (<=0,075mm)!

I highly recommend it to use tree supports (at least on the crew) - make sure to set the tree support z-distance to a multiple of 2 (layer hight) for optimal removal (keep in mind: layer hight 0.05 (crew) or 0.075mm (buggy))

---------- Over all printing recommendation (FDM) ----------

- Many parts have a flat surface -> face those to the build plate. Print slow (25mm/s)
- Detaild sides / most visible sides should be faced towards the cooling fan.
- Crew should be printed with tree supports, rotated 45Â° back (so the face is up). Support Z-distance should be a multiple of 2 to 3 of the layer hight (0.05 - 0.075mm). PRINT SLOW - Outer wall max 25mm/s. You want dem details gooood!

The most difficult parts (actually not the Gobbo Crew...):
- "RuckaBuggy_Rear_02.stl": you need to be super carefull to remove tree supports (they are needed). My test print broke on the bars while removing them. I was able to glue it back on (tip: smooth cracks with a lighter)
- "RuckaBuggy_Front_04.stl": failed 4 times while removing tree supports, combined with other parts. I needed to print this part as a single part with normal supports.
     
---------- Assembly  ----------

If you need instructions - just open all files in 3D Builder or e.g. Meshmixer. There you can look where they go.
Some parts are press fits (e.g. the wheels) you don't need to glue them, unless you print in resin (not tested).
I recommend to "dry fit" the parts with blu tack or a similar reusable putty.
